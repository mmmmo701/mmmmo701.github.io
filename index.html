Research

I love digging deep into problems. Here are some of my current and past research projects.

On Measuring the Performance of Locality Sensitive Hashing Algorithms
Locality Sensitive Hashing (LSH) is a technique of algorithms used to approximate edit distances. This research focuses on constructing a metric to measure the performance of these algorithms.
Conducted under SURA →
Theoretical Approach on Assessing the Accuracy of the Shortest Path Non-Optimal Algorithm for 2-Dimensional Grids with Obstacles
Traditional search algorithms on 2D grids guarantee to find the shortest path at the cost of quadratic time complexity. Non-optimal algorithms that trade accuracy for speed have emerged to address the issue. This research evaluates the accuracy of two non-optimal algorithms using experimental data and theoretical analysis.
